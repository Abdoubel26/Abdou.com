the universe’s biggest explosions

a supernova is a powerful explosion that marks the dramatic end of a massive star’s life. When a star exhausts its nuclear fuel, its core collapses under gravity, triggering a massive release of energy. This cosmic event can outshine entire galaxies for weeks, scattering heavy elements into space—elements crucial for forming planets and even life itself. Supernovae also leave behind fascinating remnants, such as neutron stars or black holes, making them key players in the universe’s ongoing evolution.
